## 2. Permitted uses (nominative fair use)

You may use the DDC Marks without prior written permission only
for factual, descriptive reference - for example:

- "built on OpenConstructionERP"
- "powered by OCERP"
- "imports CWICR data"
- "compatible with OpenConstructionERP 2.x"
- academic citation of the project

Such use must:

- **not alter, abbreviate, translate, or stylise** the marks
  beyond Licensor's published logo-usage guidelines;
- **not suggest** sponsorship, endorsement, affiliation, or
  partnership with Licensor where none exists;
- include a disclaimer where the product or service is not
  produced by Licensor: *"This product is not affiliated with,
  endorsed by, or sponsored by DataDrivenConstruction."*

## 3. Prohibited uses

Without prior written permission from Licensor you may NOT:

(a) use the DDC Marks in a product name, company name, or
    domain name (e.g. `openconstructionerp-cloud.io`,
    `ocerp-enterprise.com`, `cwicr-pro.io`);
(b) reproduce, modify, or stylise DDC logos or wordmarks
    beyond the nominative references described in Section 2;
(c) sell, licence, resell, or monetise OpenConstructionERP
    (including modified versions) under a name that is
    confusingly similar to a DDC Mark, whether as a hosted
    service, an on-premises distribution, or an OEM product;
(d) register DDC Marks as trademarks, service marks, domain
    names, or social-media handles in any jurisdiction;
(e) use DDC Marks in paid advertising or SEO meta-tags to
    capture traffic for a competing product;
(f) use the DDC Marks to imply that an unlicensed, closed-
    source deployment complies with the AGPL, or that a third-
    party offering is authorised by Licensor when it is not.

Use that crosses the line between permitted nominative fair use
and prohibited use is a trademark infringement under
**§§ 14-15 MarkenG** (Germany), **15 U.S.C. §§ 1114, 1125**
(United States), and **EU Regulation 2017/1001 Art. 9** (EU).

## 4. AGPL and trademarks

The AGPL-3.0-or-later licence that governs the Software
source code grants you **code rights** but **not trademark
rights**. You may fork, modify, and redistribute the code
under AGPL, but:

- you must **remove or replace** the DDC Marks from your
  user-facing UI, product name, and marketing if you
  distribute a modified version;
- retaining the DDC Marks to credit the upstream project
  (e.g. "based on OpenConstructionERP") is permitted under
  Section 2 (nominative fair use) but the modified product
  must not be named using a DDC Mark.

This trademark constraint is consistent with AGPL-3.0 itself,
which in §7 permits additional terms that prohibit misleading
use of names, logos, and trademarks.
